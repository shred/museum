Poppy 9015

Status: Not tested yet

The Poppy 9015 is one of the first color video games on the market.

Trivia

  • These devices were sold in Germany in the end of the 1970's, named Poppy 9015, Mustang 9015, Sanwa 9015, Unimex Mark IX, or TV-10 Color.
  • This Poppy came with two controllers, and two game cartridges (ball games, racing games).
  • The main console itself has no CPU. The full game logic is inside the individual cartridges.
  • Can also be powered with 6 size D batteries.

Cartridges

There have been eight game cartridges on the market. The 9015A was enclosed with the main console. I own the following cartridges:

  • ☑ 9015A: 10 ball games ("Cassette mit 10 Ballspielen") - supplied standard cartridge
  • ☑ 9015B: motorcycle racing (with four different kind of races) ("Cassette mit 4 Motorradrennen") - with original packaging
  • ☑ 9015C: tank battle ("Panzerschlacht") - with original packaging, Mustang brand (was not listed at Poppy)
  • ☑ 9015D: car racing (for 1 or 2 players) ("Rennsport") - with original packaging
  • ☑ 9015E: fight game (six different fight styles) ("Kampf-Spiel") - cartridge only
  • ☐ 9015F: hunting game ("Treibjagd")
  • ☑ 9015G: submarine ("U-Boot") - with original packaging
  • ☐ 9015H: shooting game ("Schießspiel")

Known Issues

  • Side of the case is slightly damaged from the plastic softener of the TV cable.

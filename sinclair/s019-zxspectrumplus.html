Sinclair ZX Spectrum Plus

Status: Refurbished and working

Configuration

  • Built: 1983/Q1, upgraded to 48K in 1983/Q4, upgraded to Plus in 1984 or later
  • Mainboard: Issue Two
  • CPU: Z80A
  • ULA: 6C001E-6 (date code 8343, probably made in Philippines)
  • RAM: 48KB

Trivia

  • Got this machine in a bundle with an Interface 1 and a Microdrive.
  • This machine was probably build in 1983/Q1 as 16K ZX Spectrum, and was upgraded by Sinclair to 48K around 1983/Q4.
  • Instead of a serial number, the word "Upgraded" is embossed into the case. It means that the board was originally inside a ZX Spectrum "rubber keys" case. The system was then upgraded to a "ZX Spectrum Plus" in 1984 or later. This would explain why I found an "Issue Two" board (with a twisted heatsink) in a ZX Spectrum Plus case. Sinclair sold upgrade kits, but this machine had a German warranty sticker, so the upgrade was done by Sinclair.

Known Issues

  • Speaker is very silent.
  • Cannot load from tape, signal is too weak.

Restauration Works

  • Restauration was carried out in January 2023.
  • -5V and 12V were missing. Fixed by replacing TR4 and D16. Strangely, the system was still starting without these voltages.
  • Recapping, Traco DC/DC converter, Composite Mod.
  • Case cleaned.
  • Membrane connector was broken off, so the membrane was replaced with a new replica.
  • Display had a greenish tint and a strange horizontal raster. Repaired by replacing IC14 and VR1.
  • Applied a heatsink to the ULA.
  • Perfect calibration of video quality and crystals.

History

  • 2023-01-22:
    • Replaced IC14 with a socket and a NOS LM1889N. Image quality was slightly improved.
    • Replaced VR1, which finally fixed the issue with the discoloration.
    • Reset wire was too short after recapping. Replaced with a new wire.
    • On the keyboard, the "ASDFG" keys did not respond. Replaced D3, but that didn't help. Fixed the issue by putting some tape behind the membrane contacts.
    • Applied a heatsink to the ULA.
    • Reassembled the system. Refurbishment is now completed.
  • 2023-01-21:
    • Cleaned the case
    • New keyboard membrane, as the connectors of the old one were broken off
    • Composite mod
    • Replaced 7805 with Traco Power DC/DC converter
    • -5V and 12V were bad, fixed by replacing TR4 and D16
    • Recapped
    • Diagnostics detected a 16K model. Checked all chips related to upper RAM, but eventually found a drop of solder as cause. IC16, IC17 and IC21 were detected as faulty by the Retro Chip Tester, but seem to work fine in the machine, so I kept them.
    • Display has a greenish tint and horizontal disturbances. Tried to fix it by replacing D15, TR1, TR2. Also swapped ULA, but it didn't change anything.
    • Cleaned in IPA bath. Broke off speaker when I tried to bend it up so it won't have contact to IPA, and resoldered it after the bath.
